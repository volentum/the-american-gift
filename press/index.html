THE AMERICAN GIFT: Industry Insider Eddy Perez Unleashes the Truth About What America's Mortgage Industry Owes Its Consumers

Explosive New Book Exposes "Old Guard" Exploitation and Charts a Path Forward for the New Guard — Available at TheAmericanGiftBook.com

ATLANTA, GA — The mortgage industry is about to face its reckoning. Eduardo "Eddy" Perez, Jr., the relentless CEO of EPM and visionary leader of the industry's "New Guard," is publishing the definitive expose that will force America to reckon with decades of systemic consumer exploitation in home financing.

"THE AMERICAN GIFT: The Perfect Storm – How the Mortgage Industry's Old Guard Screwed America (And Why True Brokers Are Taking It Back)" – available now at TheAmericanGiftBook.com – is the industry tell-all that insiders have been too afraid to write and consumers desperately need to read.

"My father didn't steal a rowboat and paddle through shark-infested waters for three days so I could watch this industry keep screwing people over," declares Perez, whose immigrant success story and unmatched industry credentials position him uniquely to expose truths others won't dare touch. "The American Gift isn't a privilege – it's a responsibility. And that responsibility includes telling the truth, no matter who it hurts."

The Old Guard'S Monopoly Ends Here

Perez dismantles what he calls the industry's "Old Guard" – a network he characterizes as "pale, male, stale, and frail" – that has systematically enriched itself while extracting wealth from consumers and keeping brokers weak through division and opacity.

"They want brokers fighting like gangs over territory because that keeps us fragmented and them in control," Perez explains. "Meanwhile, consumers are overpaying for phantom value – inflated rates and fees for literally nothing except a handshake and a promise. It's financial predation dressed up as customer service."

Revelations That Reshape The Industry

The American Gift exposes:

  • The Overhead Extraction: How the "Old Guard" manufactures unnecessary complexity to justify bloated fees that vanish into corporate overhead while consumers foot the bill
  • Taxes Without Representation: How G-fees imposed in 2011 were never removed – even after being repaid – and are locked in through 2030, creating a permanent tax on mortgage originators that brokers have zero power to control
  • The Exclusion Game: How information asymmetry and controlled access allow a handful of mega-lenders to maintain artificial market control while independent brokers are shut out of capital sources
  • The Red Tape Racket: How regulations ostensibly designed to "protect" consumers have multiplied costs by 400% while solving none of the actual problems – and serve primarily to protect the Old Guard's monopoly
  • The Real Villains of 2008: Why Wall Street (not mortgage brokers) engineered the crash through predatory underwriting standards, yet independent loan officers bore the regulatory burden and reputational damage

"The crash wasn't about poor underwriting by loan officers," Perez states bluntly. "It was about poor underwriting standards created by Wall Street banks. Yet somehow independent brokers – who represent the majority market share – got buried under regulations from every state, every credit bureau, Fannie, Freddie, Ginnie, FHA, VA, and USDA. That's not consumer protection. That's a monopoly enforcement mechanism."

The New Guard Is Here

But The American Gift is far more than an expose – it's a manifesto. Perez positions mortgage brokers as the industry's true "New Guard," offering consumers what they've never had: speed, transparency, choice, and competition.

"You don't have to sacrifice speed for price anymore. You don't have to choose between one mega-bank and mediocrity," Perez explains. "When 200 wholesale lenders compete for your business instead of four banks colluding, consumers win. That's why the Old Guard fights so hard to keep us divided. A unified, transparent broker community is their existential threat."

Why Now? Why This Book? Why Eddy?

As a Cuban-American who built an Inc. 5000 company while serving as Chairman of MORPAC and MBA Board Member, Perez has both the insider access and outsider moral clarity to tell this story.

His motivation is deeply rooted in his philosophy of "The American Gift" – a concept born from his father's sacrifice:

"My father escaped Cuba with nothing. He didn't come here to dream – he came here to give his family a gift. That gift is responsibility. It's the obligation to take what America provides and use it to leave your fellow man better than you found them. This book is my way of honoring that gift and holding this industry accountable for betraying theirs."

About Eduardo "Eddy" Perez, Jr.

Known throughout the industry as "The Perfect Storm," Eddy Perez, CMB, is Founder and CEO of EPM, an Inc. 5000 mortgage company based in Atlanta. He serves as Chairman of MORPAC, sits on the MBA Board, and was named one of National Mortgage Professional Magazine's "40 Most Influential Mortgage Professionals Under 40."

Born to Cuban immigrants, Perez embodies "The American Gift" – the principle that opportunity carries an obligation. His leadership philosophy centers on "The Code of Life": Honor, Duty, Discipline, Sacrifice, and Gratitude.
